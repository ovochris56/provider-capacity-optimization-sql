# Provider Capacity Optimization & Scheduling Intelligence

## Project Overview
This SQL-based healthcare analytics project evaluates provider utilization, appointment wait times, no-show behavior, referral demand, and unused provider capacity across a synthetic multi-location outpatient healthcare network.

The goal is to identify scheduling bottlenecks, improve patient access, and generate operational recommendations for healthcare leadership.

## Business Problem
Healthcare organizations often struggle to balance provider supply with patient demand. Inefficient scheduling, no-shows, and uneven provider utilization can contribute to delayed care, wasted capacity, and operational strain.

## Key Findings
- Behavioral Health emerged as the primary operational bottleneck, with the longest average wait time, highest utilization, elevated no-show rates, and strong referral demand.
- Endocrinology showed elevated wait times despite lower utilization, suggesting a possible specialist access issue.
- Medicaid patients had the highest no-show rate, indicating potential access barriers.
- Primary Care, Orthopedics, and Physical Therapy showed opportunities for improved capacity utilization.

## Recommendations
- Expand Behavioral Health scheduling capacity.
- Improve appointment adherence among high no-show populations.
- Optimize underutilized provider capacity.
- Prioritize Endocrinology access improvements.
- Develop specialty-specific scheduling strategies.
